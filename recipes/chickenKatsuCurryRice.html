Chicken Katsu Curry Over Rice

Description

Chicken Katsu Curry is a comforting Japanese dish that combines crispy, golden-fried chicken cutlets with a rich, savory curry sauce served over fluffy steamed rice. The contrast of crunchy panko-coated chicken and velvety curry creates a deeply satisfying meal that’s both hearty and full of flavor. It’s a crowd-pleasing favorite that’s surprisingly simple to make at home.

The curry sauce is mild, slightly sweet, and aromatic, simmered with onions, carrots, and potatoes until tender. Paired with freshly fried katsu and warm rice, it’s a complete meal perfect for weeknights or a cozy weekend dinner.

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 ½ cups panko breadcrumbs
  • Oil for frying
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 3 cups water or chicken broth
  • 1 block Japanese curry roux (such as Golden Curry or Vermont Curry)
  • Steamed white rice, for serving
  • Optional: sliced green onions or pickled ginger for garnish

Steps

  1. Pound the chicken breasts to an even ½-inch thickness, then season both sides with salt and pepper.

  2. Dredge each chicken breast in flour, dip into the beaten eggs, and coat thoroughly with panko breadcrumbs; set aside.

  3. Heat oil in a deep skillet or pot to 350°F (175°C) and fry the breaded chicken until golden brown and fully cooked, about 5–6 minutes per side. Transfer to a wire rack and let rest.

  4. In a separate pot, sauté the sliced onion until softened.

  5. Add the carrots and potatoes, stirring for 2–3 minutes before pouring in the water or chicken broth.

  6. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the vegetables are tender, about 15 minutes.

  7. Add the curry roux blocks, stirring until dissolved and the sauce thickens. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes.

  8. Slice the cooked chicken katsu into strips.

  9. Serve a mound of steamed rice on a plate or bowl, ladle the curry sauce over the rice, and place the sliced katsu on top.

  10. Garnish if desired and enjoy!
