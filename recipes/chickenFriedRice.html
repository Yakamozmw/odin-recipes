Chicken Fried Rice

Description

This chicken fried rice is a quick, flavorful stir-fry that combines tender chicken, fluffy rice, colorful vegetables, and savory seasonings to create a satisfying meal in under 30 minutes. It’s perfect for using leftover rice, customizable with your favorite veggies, and delivers that takeout-style taste right from your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked, chilled rice
  • 1 lb chicken breast, diced
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Sliced green onions (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat and cook the chicken until browned and fully cooked; remove and set aside.

  2. Add the remaining oil to the pan and sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant.

  3. Stir in the peas and carrots and cook until heated through.

  4. Push the veggies to one side of the pan, pour the beaten eggs into the empty space, and scramble until cooked.

  5. Add the chilled rice to the pan, breaking up any clumps, and mix well with the eggs and vegetables.

  6. Return the cooked chicken to the pan and pour in the soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), and sesame oil; stir to combine.

  7. Cook for another 2–3 minutes, adjust seasoning with salt and pepper, and garnish with green onions before serving.
