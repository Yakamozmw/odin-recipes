Chicken Fried Rice

Description

This classic homemade lasagna is warm, comforting, and layered with rich tomato meat sauce, creamy ricotta, stretchy mozzarella, and tender pasta. It’s the kind of dish that fills the house with a cozy aroma and satisfies a crowd—perfect for Sunday dinner, potlucks, or whenever you want something hearty and impressive with minimal fuss.

Baked until bubbling and golden, this lasagna delivers balanced flavor in every bite. You can prep it ahead, freeze it, or customize it with vegetables or different cheeses. Once you try this version, it may become your go-to comfort food staple.

Ingredients

For the Meat Sauce

  • 1 lb (450 g) ground beef (or half beef, half Italian sausage)
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3–4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 (28-oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (15-oz) can tomato sauce
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional, balances acidity)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • ½ tsp salt, plus more to taste
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

For the Cheese Layer

  • 15 oz (425 g) ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tsp dried parsley or Italian seasoning
  • Pinch of salt and pepper

Other Ingredients

  • 9–12 lasagna noodles (regular or oven-ready)
  • 3 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Extra grated Parmesan for topping (optional)

Steps

  1. Make the sauce:

    Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook until softened, about 3–4 minutes. Add garlic and cook 30 seconds more. Add ground beef and cook until browned; drain excess fat if needed.

  2. Simmer:

    Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, sugar, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Reduce heat and simmer for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. Prepare the noodles:

    If using regular noodles, boil them according to package directions until al dente. Drain and lay flat to prevent sticking. (Skip this step if using oven-ready noodles.)

  4. Mix the cheese filling:

    In a bowl, combine ricotta, egg, Parmesan, parsley or Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Mix until smooth.

  5. Assemble the lasagna:

    Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9×13-inch baking dish. Layer noodles on top. Spread ⅓ of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, then add a layer of sauce and a sprinkle of mozzarella. Repeat for 2–3 layers total. End with sauce and a generous layer of mozzarella and Parmesan.

  6. Bake:

    Cover with foil (avoid touching the cheese). Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake an additional 20–25 minutes until bubbling and golden.

  7. Rest and serve:

    Let the lasagna rest for 10–15 minutes before slicing to allow it to set. Serve warm.
